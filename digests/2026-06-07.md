## Top Stories

**1. OpenClaw 2026.6.2 — Reliability and Security Hardening (Imp 3)**

OpenClaw shipped its second patch release of the June cycle on June 6, 2026, landing a broad set of channel reliability and security fixes. Plugin and skill installs now route through an operator install policy instead of the old dangerous-code scanner path, tightening the supply-chain posture for self-hosted deployments. Telegram, Feishu, Discord, and WhatsApp delivery paths received hardening around duplicate transcript mirrors, approval allowlists, streamed-final previews, and poll modifiers. Agent and CLI-backed runtimes also recover more cleanly from interrupted tool calls and stale session bindings. This is an incremental but solid maintenance release that stabilizes the 2026.6.x branch ahead of whatever the team has planned for late June.

Source: [Release openclaw 2026.6.2 · openclaw/openclaw](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/releases/tag/v2026.6.2)

**2. VentureBeat: NanoClaw Repositions as Enterprise "Second Brain" (Imp 3)**

VentureBeat ran a feature on NanoCo AI — the startup behind NanoClaw — that marks a meaningful shift in how the project is being positioned commercially. The article spotlights Singapore Foreign Minister Vivian Balakrishnan's public endorsement, where he described the agent as his "second brain" and said he wouldn't "dare switch it off," framing NanoClaw not just as automation tooling but as a persistent, context-carrying professional AI layer for individual workers. NanoCo is now explicitly pitching enterprise buyers on a one-to-one employee AI model — each worker gets a continuously updated assistant that shadows their workflows across Slack, Teams, and other messaging surfaces. The combination of the $12M seed (May) and this brand repositioning suggests NanoCo is moving fast from open-source curiosity to enterprise sales motion.

Source: [NanoClaw's creators are turning the secure, open source AI agent harness into an enterprise 'second brain' — VentureBeat](https://venturebeat.com/orchestration/nanoclaws-creators-are-turning-the-secure-open-source-ai-agent-harness-into-an-enterprise-second-brain)

**3. OpenClaw Code-Reuse Accountability Story Surfaces — Let's Data Science (Imp 2)**

A piece on Let's Data Science (June 6) reported that Gavriel Cohen, creator of NanoClaw, discovered his own original code appearing inside the OpenClaw codebase without attribution. The article frames this as a broader "AI agent accountability problem" — as harnesses proliferate and fork, provenance questions are becoming harder to ignore. The story is unlikely to derail OpenClaw's momentum on its own, but it adds to a pattern of friction between the two projects and gives NanoClaw's security-and-integrity narrative another data point. Watch whether the OpenClaw maintainers respond publicly.

Source: [OpenClaw Code Reuse Exposes AI Agent Accountability Problem — Let's Data Science](https://letsdatascience.com/news/openclaw-code-reuse-exposes-ai-agent-accountability-problem-49cf9a0b)
